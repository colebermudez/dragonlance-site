Palanthas

Palanthas Gazetteer

Palanthas, the capital of Solamnia, serves as a crucial maritime hub due to its location at the mouth of the Vingaard River, facilitating trade routes between the inland and other parts of the continent. The protective natural geography, including the Vingaard Mountains and the New Sea, fortifies the city against invasions and sea storms.

The city's history is marked by periods of great prosperity and dire conflict. It has survived sieges, magical calamities, and the political turmoil of ages, including the significant impact of The Cataclysm that reshaped the world of Krynn. Palanthas' libraries and scholars have played a pivotal role in preserving the history and knowledge of Krynn through turbulent times.

The city's governance is characterized by a complex interplay between the Senate, composed of representatives from the city's powerful families, and the influence of the Knights of Solamnia. This balance of power has shaped the cities policies and its approach to both internal and external affairs for quite a while. It was only a few years after The Cactaclysm that the Senate was formed. It is unclear when exactly they were handed full control from the King.

Palanthas Locations

A Taste of Silvanost

A unique restaurant owned by Solamna Farseer, and eldery Silvanesti elven woman. She also follows an ancient tradition of telling stories to her guests while they eat.

Blossoms

An Herbalist shop in the Merchandising District. Owned by a barbarian woman named Petalfoot. The place to go for a potion or powder.

Gardens of Grace

In the new city near the Three Moons. Many elves visit the gardens while in Palanthas.

Gilbenstock Mines and Minerals Survey

A business located on the edge of the west side of the New City. The owner, Gilbenstock, hires out individuals to first survey the mine and then to dig it with one of his gnomish inventions like the Iron Dragon.

Great Library of Palanthas

The biggest library in Solamnia if not all of Ansalon. The library is three stories high, the main entrance is in the front. Semi-circular steps lead to the large double doors of glass set in bronze. Although the structure was built with the same white stone as was used with many of the other magnificent buildings of the Old City, it is unknown just how old the library is. What is known, however, is that the Great Library is home to the Aesthetics and it was home of the founder of the Aesthetics, Astinus the Chronicler.

This library is a vase repository of knowledge on any subject, and also contains the complete history of Krynn, all meticulously written down by Astinus and the Aesthetics. Admittance is free to all who come (exept Kender). At night, a side entrance is used in place of the front, which is locked. Inside, the rooms are lined floor to ceiling with bookshelves. Desks and chairs are conveniently provided throughout the library.

Here and There

A Cartographer's shop owned by Wes Songline near the waterfront. Said to be the best maps in Solamnia. Filled from floor to roof with all kinds of maps.

Old City Wall

An ancient fortification built to protect Palanthas. The wall was built in the Age of Might by dwarven engineers and is in fact, two walls. Each wall is thirty feet high, that surround a deep ditch filled with mud and stagnant water. Seven of the eight major avenues run through the gates in the wall which are guarded by minarets and 300 foot-tall towers. The towers are fortresses in their own right with battlements circling the structures at fourty and sixty feet. The towers als have armories and doezens of slits for archers to shoot from.

One-Eyed Kate's Fish Market

The only place in Palanthas can one find as varied a collection of frest fish as in One-Eyed Kate's Fish Market. Located in a massive warehouse, this shop, or rather it's owner, is said to be associated with some shady organization. The shop is easily recognizable by the sign right in front of it that has a large fish with only one eye.

Sacred Objects

A shop specializing in religious items. Few people visited this shop but in recent days, sales have gone up.

Second Skin

A tailor shop, owned by Garreth Hemsquare. The clothes leave a lot to be desired but they have loyal customers nonetheless.

Shoikan Grove

A grove of trees that guards the Tower of High Sorcery. Palanthas gives it a wide berth due to its magical qualities. The only way to pass through is with the use of a nightjewel given by the Master of the Tower of Palanthas.

Temple of Hiddukel

An underground temple that can be accessed via sewers

Temple of Kiri-Jolith

One of the loftiest sanctuaries in the city. It is lined on both sides of its house of worship by two rows of marble columns. The cieling is so high that the candles that light the temple can not reach the top, leaving it dark at night. Behind the columns are alcoves. Ther eis a Hall of Priestesses where all the women of the temple live. There are two High Priestesses and two High Priests.

Temple of Paladine

Located North of the Shoikan Grove, and West of the Central Plaza. The Temple is made of white marble and surrounded by beautiful, well-maintained gardens.

Temple of Shinare

The Philosopher's Stone

An Alchemy shop owned by Mac Oremann. It gets its name from the ingredient that alchemists use to turn lead into gold.

The Third Eye

A mere fifty feet from Shoikan Grove lies the only shop in the neighborhood. Those who want to know about their future visit here. THe shop is owned by a young female Minotaur names Soothtounge and it is said her predictions are never wrong.

The Three Moons

Near Shoikan Grove where abandoned buildings give wawy to shops and residences lies this unassuming shop. The only thing hinting at the function of this building is a small sign featuring the Three Moons of Krynn. The Three Moons is owned by Mistress Jenna. Whithin, one can find all kinds of spell components as well as magic items of a more powerful nature such as rings, staves, and scrolls. Mistress Jenna no longer runs the shop herself, her duties as head of the Conclave keep her too busy to do so. Instead she leaves the day-to-day operations to her apprentice.

The Whetstone

During the Dark Knight's reign of Palanthas, owning and selling any weapon of war was illegal. In spite of this, some Palanthias specialized in selling contraband weapons. The Whetstone, owned by a former sorcerer named Laslo Reaver was one of these places. Most come here now to have their blades sharpened to perfection that merely lightly running it across the skin will cause one to bleed.

Shrine of Takhisis

A monument built by the Dark Knights after they took control of Palanthas. Sir Elstone Kinsaid didn't want to destroy the Temple of Paladine due to its importance to the people of Palanthas. The shrine is made of black granite and adorned with a brass sculpture of Takhisis in dragon form. The Shrine is now seen as nothing more than an ancient relic that, albiet barely, adds to the city's history. One unlucky, more than likely junior, Knight of Solamnia is stationed here for one week at a time with the only orders being to keep it clean and prevent any attempts to destroy or vandalize it.

University of Palanthas

Built into a gorge, the University of Palanthas lies to the northeast of Palanthas. The Scholar's Way being the only way to get to and from there. THe ten story building was founded following the death of Vinas Solamnus and the faculty has had a strained relationship with the Aesthetics because it was Astinus who once rejected the scholar's idea of including the Great Library of Palanthas in the university. Each floor contains lecture halls, study halls, laboratories, and dorm rooms. Major fields of study include literature, philosophy, military history, law, and histroy.

Curriculum and Teachings

The curriculum at the University of Palanthas under Rhea's guidance is diverse, offering studies in arcane and divine magic, history, philosophy, and the martial arts, reflecting the multifaceted nature of the Church of Seiros itself. Special emphasis is placed on the study of ancient civilizations, dragon lore, and the divine arts, areas in which the Church holds significant interest and expertise.

Rhea herself occasionally lectures on subjects close to her heart, especially those concerning the legacy of Seiros, the history of the celestial dragon, and the moral imperatives of power and leadership. Her teachings are said to be profound, moving students to pursue greatness not for personal glory but for the greater good.

The Role of the Four Saints

The influence of the Four Saints permeates the university's ethos. Each saint inspires a specific college within the university, dedicated to advancing their virtues and teachings:

  • College of Cethleann: Focuses on healing arts, medicine, and alchemy.
  • College of Cichol: Specializes in martial training, strategy, and the code of chivalry.
  • College of Indech: Offers studies in natural sciences, magic, and philosophy.
  • College of Macuil: Dedicated to law, justice, and governance.

Challenges and Controversies

Despite its esteemed position, Rhea's leadership and the close ties between the university and the Church of Seiros are not without their critics. Some members of the academic community argue that the Church's influence stifles free inquiry and imposes doctrinal orthodoxy over intellectual exploration. There are also rumors, spoken only in hushed tones, of a secret archive and research facility beneath the university, where forbidden magic and ancient technologies are studied.

Taverns & Inns

Taverns

Boar's Head

Their slogan is "Varousing since 300 A.C."

Bone & Four Skulls

They have a sign aptly depicting a bone and four skulls. A small common area with a few tables is usually filled with wretched looking men.

Crow's Nest

Goose and Gander Tavern

This shoddy tavern is shaped like a piece of pie due to the streets next to it. A layer of soot covers tit for bein so close to Smith's Alley. Apparently it is famous for ginger beer.

The Drookit Duck

One of the more popular taverns. Sometimes travelers won't make it past the tavern.

The Dwarven Spring

Owned by Kharzog Hammerfell, this ancient public house is known for having a pool of cold, clean wawter in the back bubbling up where the beer is chilled.

The Portal

Near Shoikan Grove in the Old City. The food is said to be delicious but the drinks are its claim to fame. Weird colors and a recipie no one can decipher makes drinking at The Portal an interesting experience.

Inns

Fist and Glove

The largest Inn in Palanthas

Golden Dory

Located on the waterfront, it is known for clean rooms and decent service.

Hale and Farewell

Owned by Lame Hale in the Old City at the end of a dark alcove near the docks. It was named after parties sailors had before they left on their ships. It is known for exotic music. Its one claim to fame is the Red Lotus. A strong alcoholic drink with a strong tart taste and a hint of berry. Anyone who drinks more than one of these will become highly suggestive to another person's suggestions, throwing caution to the wind.

Myrtal's Roost

They are known for the best steak in Palanthas

The Solace Inn

Also called Inn of the Next to the Last Home. It is owned by Big Taka. When entering, one finds a large bean-shaped common area, painted to resemble a tree trunk. On the ceiling, the beams are painted like branches. A large stone fireplace is located two-thirds of the way back with a wide, large empty space in the middle of the room.
