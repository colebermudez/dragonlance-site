Haven

Haven is one of the larger and more bustling cities in Abanasinia, located south of the quiet town of Solace. It serves as a commercial and political hub for the surrounding plains and is known for its mix of people, commerce, and growing influence in the region. In contrast to the more rural and tranquil settlements like Solace, Haven is a busy, urbanized center where trade routes converge, attracting merchants, travelers, and adventurers from across Krynn. Haven is also the capital city where the main residence of the Hresvelg Dynasty lies in the center, overlooking the city and the ocean to the east.

Architecturally, Haven is a typical medieval city with cobblestone streets, market squares, and stone buildings, many of which serve as shops, inns, and guild halls. The city has a sense of wealth and progress, as it benefits from its position as a major trading center. However, this prosperity also brings with it a sense of growing political tension and moral decay. In the earlier days, Haven was a relatively peaceful and fair-minded city, but during its earlier days, it fell under the influence of the Seeker movement, a religious cult that promises spiritual renewal but in practice becomes corrupt and oppressive. The Seekers used to dominate the political scene in Haven, using their power to manipulate the populace and further their own interests. Emperor Ionius iX, Edelgard's father, drove them out when he first came to power.

Important Locations

Blade Street

Blade Street is a north-south road running from Market Street to Haven Trade Guild in Haven. Karn the Blacksmith's shop is along this road.

Crooked Street

Crooked Street is a north-south street in Haven that borders Haven's Old Keep. It branches off of Three Forks and runs to River Way.

Cross Street

Cross Street is a street in the city of Haven, near the Haven Grand Market.

Harvest Home Fair

Harvest Home Fair is a large fair held in the city of Haven every year. The popular festival is attended by a huge number of people, and vendor hawk all kinds of wares at the fair.

Haven Arms

Haven Arms was an inn in the city of Haven as of the winter of 347 AC. It was known to have served ale almost as good as Otik Sandath's, and it was known for its scrapple (scraps of port stewed with meal, then pressed into cakes).

Haven River

The Haven River is just northwest of the city of Haven and empties into the White Rage River. Haven's docks are located along this river, which is used to transport goods to and from the city.

Haven's Vale

Haven's Vale, sometimes called just Haven Vale, is located northwest of the Haven Plains. It is separated from Darken Wood by the Haven River and helps to form the western border of the forest. A bridge was to be built over Haven River to connect the Vale to Darken Wood, but the war effort in Abanasinia has currently haulted the project.

Herbalist Street

Herbalist Street is a north-south road in central Haven. It begins in the south at the Haven Mageware Shop, traveling north through Haven Grand Market and Market Street, and finally dead ends at Haven's Old Keep. The Steel Tankard Tavern and The Ivy Inn are both located along the street.

High Hand Inn

The High Hand Tavern is a tavern in Haven that occupies the same space on Market Street as the old High Hand Inn.

Hostlers Walk

Hostlers Walk is a north-south road in western Haven. It connects to Mill Street in the south and travels north across Market Street, eventually becoming Wrackham Road. The Daft Gnome Ale House is known to reside along the street, south of Market Street on the eastern side of the road.

Market Street

Market Street is an east-west road in central Haven. Three Forks, Blade Street, Herbalist Street, and Hostlers Walk all are known to intersect with Market Street. The businesses of Sharl Weaving, the High Hand Inn, and Blackrock Bakery are located along the street. Since the street cuts right through the city, travelers can enter from the east or west and follow Market Street to the Haven Grand Market that dominates the center of the city.

Mill Street

Mill Street is a small east-west street in the far southern part of Haven, and when traveling west it leads to Hostlers Walk.

Seven Centaurs Inn

The Seven Centaurs Inn was a nice inn that was located in Haven as of 344 AC. It was known to serve hot food, hot baths, and have its own stable. The owner and whether or not it still exists are unknown.

Steel Tankard Tavern

The Steel Tankard Tavern was a tavern in Haven as of the autumn of 351 AC. It was located on the corner of Herbalist Street and an unknown street, just north of the Haven Grand Market.

The Hapee Ohgr

The Happee Ohgr (a misspelling of "The Happy Ogre") is a tavern located in Haven as of the year 344 AC. The tavern's sign appears to be purposely misspelled, and has a picture of an obviously drunken ogre on the sign. It is a smoky den with drunks in the corners or slumped over sticky tables. The tavern's owner is unknown.

The Ivy Inn

The Ivy Inn is an inn famous for having ivy completely covering the entire inn. The Ivy is located in the southeastern section of Haven, known to be a fairly decent part of town. The whitewashed stone building has a second story in the back that widens towards the front as it slopes lower to the front. Like many inns, the Ivy Inn has rooms to rent, a common room, a kitchen and a bar. The kitchen is off to the side of the main building and has a stable in the back to house its occupant's horses. Outside the inn is a green-and-white-painted sign proudly proclaiming it to be The Ivy.

The Masked Dragon

The Masked Dragon was one of the largest inns in Haven as of the year 344 AC. Minstrels would play in its walled-in courtyard and entertain people as they drank. When night came, torches were lit and put into holders in the walls. The owner is not known, nor is the date of the inn's founding.

Three Forks

Three Forks is a street in eastern Haven running from River Way in the north to Market Street in the south. Its name comes from the two roads that branch from it, one of which is Crooked Road.
