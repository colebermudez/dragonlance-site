Lemish

The current Dictator of Lemish is Lord Tyrian Shattersword, a cruel master who prefers to rule through brute force – a language the population understand just fine, having used it themselves for generations. With the coming of the war Tyrian has chosen to ally himself with the Dragonarmies, and so his keep is now adorned with the
banners of the Blue Wing.

Lemish is no city of struggling and oppressed innocents, but rather it was populated already with bandits, rogues and other opportunistic peoples out to satisfy only their own greed. Most are happy to throw their lot in with the Dragonarmies, whilst those who are not are only disappointed that a larger, stronger and crueler force has been able to take over (which they would do themselves if they were that powerful).

Roads lead into the city of Lemish from the south, north-west and north-east, each guarded by squat three-story gray towers that stand astride the roads, their parapets manned at all times Dragonarmy soldiers keeping an out for danger. All travelers are stopped at these towers so that they and their wagons can be searched before proceeding to the city. Upon a completed search, each traveler is then given a token which can be given to the guards at the city gate to show that they have been searched.

Two units of Draconians man each of these towers – one on guard along the parapets and one on the ground conducting searches. All of these has a blue crystal whistle strung
around their necks to sound an alarm.

Factions

Thieves Guild

There are three organized factions of thieves and rogues within Lemish, all competing for wealth and power. Other than the Split Silverpieces, membership or association with any of these organizations is punishable by death.

The Split Silverpieces

The only guild to have received an official sanction from the Dragonarmies to continue existing, the Split Silverpieces have made a good profit taking contracts directly from Lord Tyrian, doing the dirty work he would prefer not be tied directly to him or his armies.

The Phantoms

Before the Dragonarmies came the Phantoms made a good living as road bandits and highwaymen, but they have been forced to keep a low profile since the roads became watched. Now they are merely petty thieves and pickpockets.

The Blue Circle

Operating across the southern coast of Solamnia and Lemish, the Blue Circle are primarily out for themselves. They are primarily con artists and crooked merchants, but well armed and well trained for hunting down and forcing payment from their debtors.

The local Blue Circle are led by a Half-Elf named Lareen, who is very defensive about her group being the smallest of the Blue Circle cells in the area. She is actually pretty sympathetic to the Dragonarmies as she hates Solamnia and her own people (the Qualinesti), but she does not seek official sanction for her operations because values her
independence a lot more.

Sites of Interest

Dragon's Warehouse

A large stone warehouse no the city’s north side has been converted for use by the young Dragon Topaz, it’s interior fitted out with arid stone walls and floor to create a more comfortable environment for her, given her distaste for the woodland of Lemish. The design is such that the walls trap heat within the warehouse, yet Topaz still seems to hate the setup.

Directly adjacent to the warehouse is a richly appointed apartment with a balcony that looks out into the warehouse’s interior. This apartment is the private residence of Lukas Daerlath, a local Dragonarmy champion.

Merchants

Visitors will be hard-pressed to find an honest merchant here, and those who are not local are far more likely to encounter con-men who spot them for outsiders and charge extremely high prices for even basic necessities.

Prices in Lemish are likely to be four to five times higher than the norm, and often for products that are half the quality.

Slave Market

Though not proclaimed as a major industry, many merchants in Lemish deal in slaves. Previously these were merely the captives of bandit raids and those brought in by
pirates. Since the War of the Lance, the Dragonarmies have been importing more and more captives from across Ansalon, and this trade has flourished.

Old Temple of Kiri-Jolith

An old temple to Kiri-Jolith now lies as a desecrated ruin, the only part that hasn’t been scrawled on or smashed being the graveyard, which has been respectfully left alone (though
this is partly due to superstition). Explorers find the ruin a collection of broken walls, scattered boulders and a few surviving floors and the corners of upper levels.

The many vantage points in these ruins makes it an ideal meeting place for the Blue Circle, as they can meet and speak without prying eyes, and any intruders who do come find themselves in the perfect position to be ambushed.
